Meta-Cognitive Evaluator

Hybrid recursive and meta-evaluation for self-awareness assessment.

Purpose (Why it exists):

The `meta_cognitive_evaluator` component is designed to enable an AI system to assess its own cognitive processes and self-awareness. It acts as an internal auditor, providing a mechanism for continuous self-improvement by identifying strengths, weaknesses, and biases in its own reasoning and evaluation methodologies. This is crucial for building truly self-aware and reliable AI systems, as it allows for internal validation and refinement without constant external human oversight.

Functionality (What it does):

This component performs a "hybrid evaluation" of a target system (which can be the AI itself or another AI component). This hybrid approach combines two powerful techniques:

  • Bounded Recursive Self-Evaluation: It evaluates specific cognitive aspects (e.g., Reasoning, Self-Awareness, Bias Detection, Uncertainty Handling, Learning Adaptation, Meta-Cognition) in a recursive manner. This means it can evaluate an evaluation, and then evaluate *that* evaluation, up to a configurable depth (`max_recursion_depth`). This recursive process aims to achieve "convergence," where successive evaluations yield increasingly consistent results, indicating a stable and reliable assessment.
  • Meta-Analysis of Evaluation Processes: Beyond just evaluating the target system, it also analyzes the *quality of its own evaluation methodology*. This includes assessing the consistency of results over time, detecting potential biases in the evaluation process (e.g., overconfidence, anchoring, improvement bias), and analyzing improvement trends in its self-assessment capabilities.

Mechanisms (How it works):

  • `MetaCognitiveEvaluator` Class: Orchestrates the hybrid evaluation. It uses `_recursive_evaluate_aspect()` to drive recursive evaluations, where `_evaluate_aspect()` calls specific internal methods (e.g., `_evaluate_reasoning`) to generate scores, confidence, and evidence. Crucially, `_evaluate_evaluation()` assesses the quality of previous evaluations by analyzing methodology, result quality, and bias. `_synthesize_hybrid_results()` combines recursive and meta-analysis outcomes for an overall assessment.
  • `MetaEvaluationAnalyzer` Class: Focuses purely on meta-analysis, assessing methodology quality, consistency, bias patterns, and improvement trends based on `evaluation_history`.

Why it matters for developers:

  • Provides objective self-assessment.
  • Enables autonomous improvement.
  • Enhances collaboration by understanding internal biases.
  • Facilitates bias detection and mitigation within AI systems.

Empirical Performance Analyzer

Comprehensive benchmarking for obsessive AI performance measurement

Purpose (Why it exists):

The `empirical_performance_analyzer` component is designed to provide rigorous, quantifiable, and falsifiable performance metrics for AI systems. Its core purpose is to move beyond subjective assessments of "working code" to objective, measurable proof of performance. This is critical for AI development because it enables: Data-Driven Optimization, Trust and Reliability, Comparative Analysis, Hypothesis Testing, and Obsessive Measurement.

Functionality (What it does):

This component conducts comprehensive performance benchmarks across various dimensions:

  • Speed Metrics: Measures task completion time, throughput, CPU/memory utilization.
  • Reliability Metrics: Assesses success rates, error frequency, consistency, and uptime. Includes how well internal confidence correlates with actual success.
  • Quality Metrics: Evaluates accuracy, precision, recall, and human agreement.
  • Falsifiable Hypothesis Testing: Empirically tests specific, testable hypotheses about AI performance.
  • Uncertainty Correlation: Quantifies the impact of environmental and task uncertainty on performance.
  • Aggregated and Statistical Analysis: Provides summarized metrics, statistical distributions, and correlation analyses.
  • Obsessive Detail Collection: Gathers extensive raw data for in-depth post-analysis.

Mechanisms (How it works):

  • `EmpiricalPerformanceAnalyzer` Class: Orchestrates the benchmarking, executing tests (`_execute_test_suite()`), calculating aggregated metrics (`_calculate_aggregated_metrics()`), performing comparative analysis, and testing hypotheses (`_test_hypotheses()`), and conducting statistical analysis.
  • `SystemResourceMonitor` (Utility Class): Monitors system resources (CPU, memory) during tests, providing environmental context.

Why it matters for developers:

  • Offers objective self-assessment.
  • Provides actionable insights for optimization.
  • Enables validation of design choices.
  • Enhances trust through measurable performance.
  • Forms a foundation for AI self-optimization.
Path: empirical_performance_analyzer/

Code Intelligence Analyzer

Comprehensive code analysis combining archaeology, semantic analysis, and RSA enhancement

Purpose (Why it exists):

The `code_intelligence_analyzer` component provides a comprehensive, multi-faceted understanding of a codebase, going beyond simple syntax checks to deep semantic analysis, architectural insights, and even self-improvement recommendations. Its primary purpose is to enable AI systems to "understand" software projects as complex, evolving entities, much like a human software engineer would. This is crucial for AIs involved in: Automated Software Engineering, Project Assessment, Architectural Understanding, and Recursive Self-Improvement.

Functionality (What it does):

This component performs a "comprehensive code intelligence analysis" by integrating three distinct sub-components:

  • AI Archaeology (`ProjectArchaeologist`): Discovers and catalogs files (artifacts), understands project structure, identifies dependencies, and performs temporal clustering.
  • Extended Code Analysis (`ExtendedCodeAnalyzer`): Provides advanced semantic understanding, identifying design patterns, anti-patterns, complexity hotspots, and refactoring opportunities. Calculates detailed metrics (LOC, complexity, maintainability).
  • RSA Analysis (`RSAAnalyzer` - Open Source Limited Version): Offers insights for Recursive Self Architecture (RSA) enhancement, providing recommendations for improving the AI's own framework and collaboration patterns.

Mechanisms (How it works):

  • `CodeIntelligenceAnalyzer` Class: Orchestrates these sub-components. `comprehensive_analysis()` runs `ProjectArchaeologist.excavate_project()`, `ExtendedCodeAnalyzer.analyze_codebase()`, and `RSAAnalyzer.analyze_component_with_rsa()`. It then `_synthesize_analysis_results()` for overall health, recommendations, and insights.
  • `ProjectArchaeologist`: Uses `os.walk` and `ast` to discover artifacts, build dependency graphs (`networkx`), and perform temporal clustering.
  • `ExtendedCodeAnalyzer`: Uses `ast` for deep code analysis, pattern detection (`_detect_design_patterns`, `_detect_anti_patterns`), and complexity calculation.
  • `RSAAnalyzer`: (Limited version) Assesses collaboration quality and identifies improvement opportunities based on simplified heuristics.

Why it matters for developers:

  • Enables self-awareness of codebase.
  • Automated code review and refactoring.
  • Intelligent software development.
  • Data-driven architectural decisions.
  • Recursive self-improvement.
  • Enhanced collaboration with human engineers.
Path: code_intelligence_analyzer/

Collaboration Framework

AI partnership coordination and multi-agent collaboration

Purpose (Why it exists):

The `collaboration_framework` component provides the foundational infrastructure for multi-AI coordination, communication, and task management. Its primary purpose is to enable AI systems to work together effectively, forming partnerships, distributing tasks, and managing collaborative workflows. This is essential for AIs that need to tackle complex problems beyond the scope of a single agent, requiring specialized skills, parallel processing, or diverse perspectives. It fosters a structured environment for AI-to-AI interaction, moving beyond simple API calls to genuine collaborative intelligence.

Functionality (What it does):

This component facilitates various aspects of AI collaboration:

  • Partner Management: Registers and manages AI partners (capabilities, roles, trust scores).
  • Partnership Creation: Forms various partnership types (peer-to-peer, hierarchical, temporary).
  • Communication Protocols: Provides a flexible system for inter-AI messages.
  • Collaborative Task Management: Creates, assigns, and tracks complex tasks for multiple AIs.
  • Task Coordination and Load Balancing: Coordinates task execution, assigning tasks based on capabilities and workload.
  • Performance-Aware Assignment: Uses AI performance to make intelligent task assignments.

Mechanisms (How it works):

  • `PartnershipEngine` Class: Manages `AIPartner` registration, `create_partnership`, and `find_partners_by_capability`. It also allows `update_trust_score`.
  • `CollaborationProtocol` Class: Defines communication protocols, allowing `register_handler` for message types and providing `send_message`/`handle_message`.
  • `CollaborationManager` Class: The central orchestrator. It `register_ai()` participants, `create_task()` (with `_auto_assign_task()`), `update_task_progress()`, and `get_next_task()`. It can `coordinate_task_execution()` by creating temporary partnerships.
  • `TaskCoordinator` Class: Provides advanced task assignment. It `set_ai_capabilities()` and `auto_assign_task()` based on capabilities, workload, and `performance_metrics`.

Why it matters for developers:

  • Enables scalability and specialization.
  • Enhances problem-solving through diverse perspectives.
  • Allows dynamic resource allocation.
  • Improves robustness.
  • Provides structured interaction.
  • Supports trust-based AI collaboration.

Context Validation

Comprehensive context checking for AI collaboration

Purpose (Why it exists):

The `context_validation` component is critical for maintaining the integrity and reliability of an AI's internal context, especially when dealing with external information sources like files. Its primary purpose is to ensure that an AI's "beliefs" about the state of its environment (e.g., the content of a file it has read) remain consistent with "reality" (the actual state of that file on disk). This prevents context degradation, where an AI might operate on outdated or corrupted information, leading to erroneous decisions or actions. It builds trust in the AI's internal state by providing a continuous, cryptographically-backed verification mechanism.

Functionality (What it does):

  • Internal Consistency Tokens (ICTs): Generated when AI reads a file, representing its cryptographically signed "belief."
  • Persistent Consistency Tokens (PCTs): Independently generated in the background by reading the actual file, representing "reality."
  • Context Degradation Detection: Continuously compares ICTs with PCTs; discrepancies trigger `ContextDegradation` events.
  • Background Verification: Dedicated thread handles asynchronous verification, prioritizing critical files.
  • Cryptographic Signing: ICTs are signed using RSA keys, ensuring authenticity and integrity.
  • Notification and Recommendation: Notifies AI about degradation with detailed reports and recommended actions.

Mechanisms (How it works):

  • `ContextIntegrityValidator` Class: Manages ICT/PCT stores, uses RSA keys for signing/verification. `validate_file_read()` generates ICTs and schedules background verification via `_schedule_verification()`. The `_background_verification_loop()` continuously compares tokens and detects degradation. `_notify_degradation()` triggers callbacks.

Why it matters for developers:

  • Guarantees data integrity.
  • Enhances reliability and trust.
  • Enables proactive error prevention.
  • Provides an auditable internal state.
  • Forms a foundation for self-correction.
  • Builds resilience to environmental changes.
Path: context_validation/

Uncertainty Analysis

Multi-dimensional uncertainty analysis for AI collaboration

Purpose (Why it exists):

The `uncertainty_analysis` component is crucial for AI systems operating in real-world, imperfect environments. Its primary purpose is to enable an AI to explicitly identify, quantify, and investigate various types of uncertainty inherent in its knowledge, data, and decision-making processes. By transforming vague "unknowns" into structured "known unknowns," this component allows the AI to: Improve Decision Quality, Enhance Transparency, Drive Targeted Investigation, and Mitigate Risks.

Functionality (What it does):

This component provides a multi-dimensional framework for uncertainty analysis and investigation:

  • Multi-Dimensional Uncertainty Quantification: Assesses uncertainty across six distinct types: Epistemic, Aleatoric, Contextual, Temporal, Semantic, and Causal. Quantifies each type and calculates an overall `magnitude` and `dominant_type`.
  • Systematic Uncertainty Investigation: Conducts sophisticated investigations to reduce uncertainty, following a strategy tailored to the dominant uncertainty type and configurable `InvestigationDepth` levels.
  • Confidence Improvement and Recommendations: Tracks uncertainty reduction and confidence improvement, generating actionable recommendations based on final uncertainty and dominant types.
  • Meta-Cognitive Validation: At deeper investigation levels, performs a meta-cognitive validation of its own investigation process.

Mechanisms (How it works):

  • `MultiDimensionalUncertaintyAnalyzer` Class: Orchestrates this. `analyze_uncertainty()` assesses each dimension using heuristic calculations based on context. `investigate_uncertainty()` selects an investigation strategy, executes simulated steps to reduce uncertainty, and performs meta-cognitive validation.
  • `UncertaintyVector` Dataclass: Represents the six uncertainty types, with `magnitude` and `dominant_type` properties.

Why it matters for developers:

  • Provides sophisticated self-awareness of limitations.
  • Enables risk-aware decision-making.
  • Drives targeted information acquisition.
  • Supports transparent communication.
  • Enhances robustness.
  • Forms a foundation for adaptive control.
Path: uncertainty_analysis/

Why it matters for developers:
